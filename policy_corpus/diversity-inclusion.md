## 1. Introduction
### 1.1 Purpose
NaijaStay Recommender (NSR) is committed to fostering an inclusive environment that respects and reflects Nigeria's profound ethnic, religious, cultural, gender, age, and socioeconomic diversity. This policy ensures our hotel recommendation and booking platform delivers fair, unbiased, and culturally sensitive suggestions to all users, while promoting diversity within our workforce and operations.

Nigeria is home to over 250 ethnic groups (major: Hausa-Fulani ~29%, Yoruba ~21%, Igbo ~18%, others including Ijaw, Tiv, Fulani subgroups) and a religious landscape divided roughly between Islam (~50-56%), Christianity (~43-48%), and ethnic/traditional faiths (~1-10%). Our AI-driven recommendations must never perpetuate exclusion, stereotypes, or discrimination based on ethnicity, religion, region (North/South divides), gender, age, or other protected characteristics.

### 1.2 Scope
Applies to all NSR employees, contractors, partners, algorithms, data practices, marketing, and user interactions. This policy supports compliance with the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA), GAID 2025 (fairness, non-discrimination in data processing), the 1999 Constitution (Sections 42 on non-discrimination), and international best practices.

## 2. Commitment to Diversity and Inclusion
NSR pledges to:
- Deliver equitable hotel recommendations that respect users' diverse backgrounds (e.g., halal-friendly options for Muslim users, family-oriented or women-safe facilities without bias).
- Avoid amplifying societal divides (e.g., no preferential ranking based on ethnic/religious proxies like region unless user-explicitly requested).
- Build and maintain a workforce that mirrors Nigeria's diversity to better understand user needs.
- Promote inclusive language, imagery, and practices in all communications and features.
- Continuously monitor and mitigate bias in our systems to ensure fairness.

This aligns with our core values (see Company Overview) and supports business goals: diverse teams innovate better, and inclusive services build user trust and loyalty across Nigeria.

## 3. Inclusive Recommendation Practices
### 3.1 Fairness in AI Recommendations
Our recommendation engine (see Hotel Recommendation Algorithm Procedures) incorporates safeguards against bias:
- **Diverse Training Data**: Use balanced datasets representing Nigeria's ethnic/religious/regional mix; supplement with localized inputs where gaps exist.
- **Bias Mitigation Techniques**:
  - Weighted scoring avoids over-penalizing underrepresented hotels (e.g., Northern vs. Southern options).
  - Rule-based overrides for sensitive preferences (e.g., gender-neutral defaults; explicit halal/Christian-friendly filters only on user request).
  - Post-processing re-ranking to promote diversity (e.g., ensure top results include varied options unless user filters narrow it).
- **Protected Attributes**: Ethnicity, religion, region treated as sensitive; no direct use in scoring unless consented and necessary.
- **User Control**: Allow users to specify preferences (e.g., "mosque nearby," "church proximity," "family-oriented in multi-ethnic area") without assumptions.

### 3.2 Monitoring for Bias
- Quarterly audits using fairness metrics (e.g., demographic parity, equal opportunity across simulated ethnic/religious/regional user cohorts).
- Disparate impact analysis on recommendation outputs (e.g., compare satisfaction/ranking for Northern Muslim vs. Southern Christian profiles).
- Annual third-party review of ML models for hidden biases.
- User feedback loops (in-app surveys) to detect perceived unfairness.

## 4. Employee Training and Responsibilities
### 4.1 Mandatory Training
- All employees complete annual Diversity & Inclusion training (2-4 hours), covering:
  - Nigeria's ethnic/religious landscape and cultural sensitivities in travel/hospitality.
  - AI bias risks (e.g., Western-centric data leading to poor Northern/Nigerian recommendations).
  - Inclusive language and unconscious bias recognition.
  - Handling diverse user complaints (e.g., religious accommodation issues).
- Developers/ML team receive specialized training on fairness-aware ML (e.g., bias detection tools, diverse dataset curation).
- New hires complete induction module within first month.

### 4.2 Responsibilities
| Role/Group                  | Key Responsibilities |
|-----------------------------|----------------------|
| Leadership (CEO/CTO)        | Champion policy; allocate resources for audits/training. |
| HR & Compliance             | Deliver training; handle reports of discrimination/bias. |
| Product/Engineering Teams   | Embed fairness in features; conduct bias checks pre-release. |
| Customer Service            | Respond sensitively to diversity-related feedback. |
| All Employees               | Report concerns; use inclusive practices daily. |

Violations (e.g., discriminatory language, ignoring bias flags) follow disciplinary process per Employee Code of Conduct.

## 5. Reporting and Continuous Improvement
- Anonymous reporting channel (ethics@naijastay.com or in-app form) for bias/discrimination concerns.
- Annual policy review incorporating audit results, user feedback, and regulatory updates (e.g., NDPC guidance on AI fairness).
- Metrics tracked: % diverse workforce, bias audit scores, user satisfaction by demographic cohorts.
- Partnerships with NGOs/hotels promoting inclusive tourism in Nigeria.
