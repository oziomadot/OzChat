## 1. Introduction
### 1.1 Purpose
This policy defines how NSR delivers excellent customer service and handles complaints for users seeking hotel recommendations and bookings in Nigeria. It ensures timely, empathetic, and fair responses, building trust while complying with Nigerian regulations (e.g., FCCPC consumer protection guidelines, NDPA 2023 for data-related complaints).

### 1.2 Scope
Applies to all user interactions via in-app chat, email (support@naijastay.com), WhatsApp Business (+234-XXX-XXXXXX), and any future channels. Covers pre-booking queries, recommendation issues, booking/payment problems, post-stay complaints, refunds, and data/privacy concerns.

| Channel              | Availability                  | Primary Use Cases                          | Notes |

## 3. Service Level Agreements (SLAs)
SLAs are realistic, measurable, and tiered by severity (informed by Nigerian tech support norms and travel sector expectations):

| Severity Level | Description                                      | First Response SLA | Target Resolution SLA | Examples |
|----------------|--------------------------------------------------|--------------------|-----------------------|----------|
| Critical      | Booking failure, payment deducted but no confirmation, data breach suspicion | 1 hour (during business hours); 4 hours off-hours | 4 hours              | Urgent refund needed, hotel overbooking |
| High          | Wrong recommendation causing inconvenience, refund requests | 4 hours           | 24 hours             | Preference mismatch (e.g., family vs. solo) |
| Medium        | General queries, minor app issues                | 12 hours          | 48 hours             | Update profile, amenity clarification |
| Low           | Feedback, non-urgent suggestions                 | 24 hours          | 5 business days      | General app improvement ideas |

- **Business Hours**: Mon–Fri 8 AM–8 PM WAT; weekends/holidays reduced staffing.
- **Measurement**: Tracked via ticketing system (e.g., Zendesk/Intercom integration); monthly reports to leadership.
- **Breach Handling**: If SLA missed, auto-apology + priority escalation; credits/refunds considered for repeated breaches.

## 4. Escalation Matrix
Structured escalation ensures issues reach appropriate levels quickly:

| Level | Role/Team                     | When to Escalate                          | Response Time After Escalation | Contact/Notes |
|-------|-------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|--------------------------------|---------------|
| Level 1 | Customer Service Agent       | Initial handling; most queries resolved here | N/A                           | In-app/WhatsApp/email |
| Level 2 | Senior Support Specialist    | Unresolved after 4–12 hours (per SLA) or high severity | Within 2 hours                | Assigned via ticket system |
| Level 3 | Team Lead / Product Manager  | Complex issues (e.g., algorithm bias, payment gateway failures) or repeated complaints | Within 4 hours                | Involves engineering if needed |
| Level 4 | Head of Customer Experience / CTO | Critical systemic issues, legal/privacy complaints, media/PR risk | Immediate (within 1 hour)     | CEO notified if reputational |
| Level 5 | Executive (CEO)              | Extreme cases (mass complaints, regulatory involvement) | As required                   | Final decision-maker |

- **Process**: Agent documents reason for escalation in ticket notes; notifies next level via @mention/Slack. Customer updated on status.
- **Re-escalation**: If no progress in agreed time, auto-notify higher level.

## 5. Complaint Documentation and Continuous Improvement
- **Requirements**: All interactions logged in centralized ticketing system with:
  - User ID, timestamp, channel, issue description, actions taken, resolution/outcome.
  - Screenshots/voice notes (with consent per Data Privacy Policy).
  - Root cause analysis for recurring issues.
- **Feedback Loops**: Post-resolution surveys (NPS-style); monthly analysis of top complaints (e.g., recommendation accuracy, Northern vs. Southern hotel availability).
- **Training**: Agents trained quarterly on empathy, cultural sensitivity (Nigeria's diversity), de-escalation, and product knowledge.
- **Improvement**: Quarterly reviews; update policy based on trends, user feedback, and benchmarks (e.g., aim to improve First Contact Resolution >70%).
